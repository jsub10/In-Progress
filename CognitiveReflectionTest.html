Decision researchers may neglect cognitive ability because they are more\n
interested in the average effect of some experimental manipulation. On this view,\n
individual differences (in intelligence or anything else) are regarded as a\n
nuisance\xe2\x80\x94as just another source of \xe2\x80\x9cunexplained\xe2\x80\x9d variance. Second, most studies\n
are conducted on college undergraduates, who are widely perceived as fairly\n

study something they do not \xef\xac\x81nd interesting, that is not perceived to vary much\n

The Cognitive Re\xef\xac\x82ection Test (CRT)\n
Many researchers have emphasized the distinction between two types of cog-\n
